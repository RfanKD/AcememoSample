Alpha System Testing

We have tested our prototype earlier mostly with elder people and we got some good feedback. This time we wanted to cover an untouched persona we initially wrote for the project objective: alcoholic & drug user. We tested our alpha system with Paulina who is 20 years old and she is a drug user. She has previously reported having short term memory problems due to her continuous drug use. We were lucky enough to find this person through our friends when we were mentioning about our application to them. She fits in the persona perfectly, and as a young and experienced mobile device user, she let us see our app in a whole different view. We used the usability testing plan we had prepared earlier and used for the previous test.

Test Results & Observations

Going through the usability test in order, she relatively had minimal difficulty navigating through the app compared to previous testers.

Task 1: She successfully connected to Facebook and started the app. It took her about 5 seconds to do so.

Task 2: She took her time to read the instructions and pressed Start Playing shortly after.

Task 3: Here, she first looked puzzled when she saw the number but then decided to not touch the level increase decrease buttons. As per our instructions, we asked her to start with 1 pair, so she pressed Start Game without much thought.

Task 4: Here, she didn’t even spend a second to read the Tutorial text for Level 1. She knew she had to drag and drop the pictures; however, since the pictures are small, she had difficulty doing so.

Task 5: She continued to next level, and completed it. We asked her to play the same level again as per our instructions, so she pressed Replay Level and played again.

Task 6: She continued to next level and after dealing with small pictures to drag & drop for a while, she completed the level. She failed the level as per our instructions. She then viewed her Correct Answers without any problem.

Task 7: After we asked her to play again but starting with level 3, she pressed “Restart Game” and pressed the increase arrow in the first screen until it reached 3.

Comparison with Earlier Results

The different factor with this usability testing was that we focused on entirely different persona. Our tester was young and since she was very used to interacting with mobile user interfaces, she gave us some very useful feedback. She was different in the sense that while earlier testers who were elder spent considerable amount of time trying to understand a mobile interface they have never seen before, she was at the level to give us the feedback for some improvements we were already considering after the demo with our TA.

Interface Evolution from Alpha

We have incorporated some of the feedback we had both from the TA when we gave the alpha demo and the user testing. The improvements on alpha are stated below with their justification on their effects on the usability goals.

1. Increase the Size of the Pictures

We heard back from both our testers and the TA that the pictures in our app were sometimes too small, especially when using a profile picture where the details were too minute to identify. In order to have the player concentrate on the goal of the game rather than the details of a profile picture, we decided to increase the size of the images. By doing so, we also made it easier for the player to select and drag the images across the screen. This accommodates many more players, seeing as many elderly patients who will be using the app have larger fingers, causing their touch to be less precise than most young players. Also, since our one of our usability goals states “User interface interactions take minimal time to complete”, this was a necessary change.

2. Improve Aesthetics

Similar to the above change, improving the aesthetics of our app will force the player to concentrate more on the game, than on trying to figure out what to do or click next. Firstly, by improving the loading screen, the player will be much less bored while waiting for the Facebook images to load. Secondly, improving the results screen by adding formatting and color, will make the page more intuitive, and the player will not be obliged to read all the words on the screen if they choose not to. Finally, by only having 5 pairs of people and their likes on the game playing screen, it will be easier for the player to drag and drop with more space on the screen. Additionally, we have also designed an icon and a logo specifically for this app. This change was necessary because of the feedback we got from the TA and our tester. We were aware of it before, but other major changes that focused on reflecting on the usability goals delayed us focusing on aesthetics.

3. Best Score

By adding a “Best Score” statistic to our game, a player will be more motivated to improve their score at a later date, rather than just playing the game over and over again to improve their memory. With this score, the player and the therapist will be able to track their progress over time and see how often they are able to improve their best score. Also, having a “Best Score” will encourage the player to continue playing more levels rather than giving up earlier than need be. This improvement was inspired by the post-test questionnaire comment from our tester. She said “It would be great if I could see my top score. That way I would challenge myself to improve.” We were also thinking about this earlier as one of our usability goals states that “User feels encouraged by the system to improve her short term memory”, so this change was necessary to provide that encouragement factor.

4. Improve Facebook data loading time

Finally, loading time is something that many players do not have the patience to wait for. By decreasing the loading time for pulling data from Facebook, we are decreasing the amount of time that a player has to wait at each level before beginning to play. A shorter wait time will encourage the player to play for longer rather than be discouraged and frustrated from having to wait every time they want to play a new level. By improving our Facebook data loading time, we targeted our usability goal which states “User interface interactions take minimal time to complete”.
